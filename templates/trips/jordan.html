The trip in a nutshell

An overnight stay under the stars that point the way to the caravans remind us where we are and capture our eyes and minds. The miracle goes on for hours and miles... At Petra we lose our sense of distance or time. And as we move on, the landscape as well as the monuments and wonders of nature become even more mysterious.

It is, you see, a Globus journey, so unlike the usual. Truly, is there a more memorable experience than an overnight stay under the infinite stars visible from a desert, but also a dinner "cooked" below ground? The first travelers who experienced it have conveyed their enthusiasm. We're waiting to hear from you...

What to expect

This trip is a complete travelogue in Jordan, starting from its capital Amman and continuing with the archaeological site of Gerasa, an important Greek city that belonged to the Decapolis of "Hellenic Syria". We come to the castle of Aljoun. We visit Madaba, Jordan's most important Christian center, the biblically significant Mount Nebo and Bethany, the place where, according to the Scriptures, Jesus is believed to have been baptized. The stunningly beautiful Petra is definitely one of the highlights of the trip, the Nabataean state with its impressive monuments that seem to take us back in time. This is followed by a "safari" in specially adapted vehicles (with a cart) to the "surreal" Wadi Ram Valley with its magical mountains of granite and colorful sand, carved by the greatest sculptor, the wind, and an overnight stay in a luxurious Bedouin tent in the desert. We also come to Aqaba, the Red Sea beach resort, which is Jordan's only outlet to the sea, and end up at the Dead Sea, a stunning natural phenomenon in a magnificent landscape!
